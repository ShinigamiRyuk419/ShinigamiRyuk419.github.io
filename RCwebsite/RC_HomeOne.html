How I turn rubber into an amazing chair?


         Tires are expensive and if you got your tires from the best Tires Supplier, there is a chance of getting a long time service from your tires. However, eventually you need to change your tires many times and now probably, you are harboring a pile of tires for a long time at your home. It is time to re-use these old tires.

         No matter how many tires you got, there is always a way to re-use it. Tire rubber is very enduring because of its elasticity. Instead of throwing them off, you can use them to decorate your interior, exterior, or do something for your kids. Take a look at the brilliant ways we have in our store for you to turning your old tires into something innovative-


Tire Chair:

         It would do with both small and big tires. Take a tire (in any size), brush off the dust and stains. Paint it in your favourite colour or you can choose white so that it can match with contrast. For a more comfortable seat, cut plywood in round shaped (tire size) and fix it on the upper area of the tire. Next, add a cushion over the plywood and your tire chair is ready to sit. Tire chairs s are perfect for your outdoor space (garden, balcony). Its shape and colours will brighten up your space beautifully.

Used car tires transformed into garden pot!


         Many of the ideas that I share here can be customized to fit indoor or outdoor gardens. Consider your space, preferences, and plants when choosing and customizing recycled items for your gardening.

         You can set up a tire garden by filling a used tire with soil then planting seeds or plants of your preference. Stack the tires to create raised beds or use single tires as separate and contained planters. Add a pop of color by painting the outside of the tire planters to match your garden design or landscaping.


         While rubber tires are made of different chemicals, it’s unlikely that they will leak into the soil. Tires release these chemicals upon incineration, not when they’re dormant. Additionally, the most concerning chemicals in tires—polycyclic hydrocarbons and heterocyclic aromatic amines—are not water-soluble, so it’s highly unlikely that they will leak into your soil or veggies.
